# Second-Round Leaderboard Optimization Report

The deep, exhaustive optimization round is complete. By running 300 Bayesian tuning trials, explicitly modeling Day 48 temporal rolling OOFs, and aggressively optimizing ensembling weights, we successfully pushed the strict Time-Series Validation score to an impressive **83.4358**.

## Feature Engineering Refinement
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Geohash Coverage Breakdown**:
> - Day 48 Train Geohashes: 1,241
> - Test Geohashes: 1,190
> - **Unseen in Test**: 15 (1.26%)

Because the test set introduced geohashes completely unseen in the training split, we rigorously tested fallback encodings to prevent model degradation.
- **Mean Target Encoding**: `75.9740`
- **Median Target Encoding**: `76.1657`
- **Smoothed Target Encoding**: **`76.1866`**

**Conclusion**: We integrated Smoothed Target Encoding (`alpha=10`) into the global pipeline to shrink low-cardinality geohashes toward the global mean, mitigating overfitting and producing the strongest baseline.

## Large-Scale Hyperparameter Search (Optuna)
We executed 300 total trials across XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost (Encoded), and CatBoost (Native). Using an adaptive allocation strategy (softmax over initial 30-trial scores), Optuna shifted 156 of the remaining trials exclusively into the `CatBoost (Native)` model.

**Optimization Results (Validation R²):**
1. **LightGBM**: 77.6260
2. **XGBoost**: 80.6522
3. **CatBoost (Encoded)**: 80.6211
4. **CatBoost (Native)**: **83.3508**

> [!TIP]
> Native categorical handling in CatBoost completely destroyed the Label Encoded variant. Skipping pre-processing for `geohash`, `RoadType`, and `Weather` significantly improved CatBoost's ability to model spatial non-linearities.

## Advanced Ensembling & Correlation Analysis
We evaluated the Pearson and Spearman correlation across the three distinct OOF predictions:
- `XGB` & `LGB` (Pearson): 0.994
- `XGB` & `CAT` (Pearson): 0.993
- `LGB` & `CAT` (Pearson): 0.995

The base models were extremely highly correlated. As a direct result:
1. **Equal Weight Blend**: Score dropped to `81.0671`.
2. **SciPy Optimized Blend**: Because CatBoost Native was so drastically superior, the optimizer assigned weights of `[XGB=0, LGB=0, CAT=1]`, defaulting entirely to CatBoost.
3. **Rank Blend**: Score dropped to `80.0146`.

## The Winning Architecture: Stacking via Temporal Rolling Splits
To safely extract the marginal orthogonal signal from XGBoost and LightGBM without overfitting to CatBoost, we built a Level-2 Stacking framework.
- We used a **5-Fold TimeSeriesSplit** strictly *within* Day 48 to generate rigorous pseudo-OOF predictions.
- We passed these pseudo-OOFs to Meta-Models.
- **Ridge**: 83.3348
- **Linear Regression**: **83.4358**
- **ElasticNet**: 0.0000 (Collapsed)
